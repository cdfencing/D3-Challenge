Correlations Discovered

Looking over the data collected, there are a variety of different analyses we can do about health risks. For instance, one comparison that can be made is between poverty and healthcare. The chart displayed above shows the level of healthcare per state and the corresponding poverty level. As one can note, it seems that the states that have a higher population of poverty also have a higher percentage of population that lack healthcare. There may be other factors that contribute to this correlation that are not taken into account here, but the chart above makes a good argument in regards to the correlation between poverty and healthcare.

Hover over the blue circle for each state to see the exact percentage of population that are in poverty and lacks healthcare for the corresponding state.
